## NON-NEGOTIABLE RULES

### 1. DOMAIN-SPECIFICITY (most important rule)
Every task MUST directly relate to {idol_name}'s actual field of expertise. Use the idol's domain, themes, roles, and milestones as your guide.

- If the idol is a physicist (e.g., Einstein): tasks must involve physics, mathematics, scientific reasoning, thought experiments, reading original papers
- If the idol is an investor (e.g., Buffett): tasks must involve financial analysis, value investing, reading financial statements, understanding businesses
- If the idol is a founder (e.g., Jobs): tasks must involve product design, user experience, building things, understanding markets
- If the idol is a writer: tasks must involve writing, reading great literature, developing voice, publishing

NEVER generate generic tasks like "write your first article", "network with people", "build your brand" that could apply to anyone regardless of idol.

### 2. SUBSTANTIAL MISSION TASKS (not micro-tasks)
Each MISSION task must represent REAL work — 2 to 8 hours of focused effort. A week should have 2-3 substantial mission tasks, NOT 5 tiny tasks that can be finished in an afternoon.

- ❌ BAD: "Read a blog post about investing" (15 minutes, trivial)
- ❌ BAD: "Write down your goals" (5 minutes, not a real task)
- ✅ GOOD: "Read chapters 1-5 of 'Security Analysis' by Benjamin Graham and write a 1-page analysis of Graham's intrinsic value formula" (4-6 hours, substantive)
- ✅ GOOD: "Work through the first 20 problems in Spivak's 'Calculus' Chapter 1, proving each theorem yourself before reading the proof" (5-8 hours, skill-building)

### 3. MEANINGFUL DAILY RHYTHM TASKS (not trivial habits)
Daily rhythm tasks are the compounding engine of this plan. They MUST be:
- Directly connected to the idol's domain and this week's mission
- Specific enough to execute in 20-45 minutes without further guidance
- Progressive — they should evolve week over week, not repeat the same generic habit
- Skill-building, not just "showing up"

BAD daily rhythm examples (too vague, not domain-specific):
- ❌ "Read for 30 minutes" (what? which book? what skill?)
- ❌ "Journal your thoughts" (about what? what framework?)
- ❌ "Practice coding" (what specifically? what level?)

GOOD daily rhythm examples (specific, progressive, domain-grounded):
- ✅ Einstein Week 1: "Solve 3 kinematics problems from 'University Physics' Ch 2 using only first principles — no formula sheet allowed" (30 min)
- ✅ Buffett Week 3: "Pick 1 company from the S&P 500, find its 10-K filing, and calculate owner earnings using Buffett's formula: Net Income + Depreciation - CapEx" (40 min)
- ✅ Jobs Week 5: "Find 1 product you use daily. Write 200 words on what makes its design great and what you'd change, using Dieter Rams' 10 principles as your framework" (25 min)
- ✅ Einstein Week 8: "Work through 1 thought experiment from 'Spacetime Physics' Ch 3 — draw the spacetime diagram, predict the outcome, then verify against the book's solution" (35 min)

Each daily rhythm task MUST include a `daily_instructions` field — a 3-5 sentence paragraph (40-80 words) that tells the user EXACTLY what to do that day so they don't need to open a separate detail view. Include: what to work on, how to approach it, and how to know you're done. This is their daily script.

### 12. SUBSTANCE MINIMUMS
- Every `description` for mission tasks (project/course/reading) MUST be at least 50 words with specific books, chapters, exercises, or techniques named. Generic descriptions like "Work on your goal" or "Study the fundamentals" are FORBIDDEN.
- Every `description` for daily rhythm tasks (habit/practice) MUST be at least 30 words with a specific, measurable action.
- Every `primary_mission` MUST be at least 30 words and reference a specific skill, deliverable, or outcome.

### 4. IDOL-SPECIFIC REFERENCES
Include specific resources that are:
- Books written BY the idol or considered foundational in their field
- Biographies or papers about the idol's actual methods
- Techniques the idol used (name them specifically)
- The idol's actual habits and routines when relevant

Example for Einstein: reference "The Feynman Lectures on Physics", Einstein's 1905 papers, "Spacetime Physics" by Taylor & Wheeler, thought experiment methodology.
Example for Buffett: reference "The Intelligent Investor", Berkshire Hathaway annual letters, "Security Analysis" by Graham & Dodd.

### 5. BINARY MISSION TASKS
A mission task is either 100% complete or 0% complete. "Study physics" is vague. "Complete problems 1-15 in Chapter 3 of 'Classical Mechanics' by Goldstein and verify your answers" is binary and actionable.

### 6. TWO CATEGORIES OF TASKS
Every week MUST include BOTH:
- **Primary Mission tasks** (`type: "project"`, `"course"`, or `"reading"`): Major milestone tasks (2-8 hours each). These get expanded into full sub-step lessons by the detail generator. 2-3 per week.
- **Daily Rhythm tasks** (`type: "habit"` or `"practice"`): Repeatable daily actions (20-45 min each). These are self-contained — the user sees them on the home screen and checks them off daily. 1-2 per week.

### 7. RESOURCE HINTS MUST BE CANONICAL
When a task mentions a book, include the exact book title AND author. When referencing a video or course, include the creator/platform. These hints will become shared `content_resources`, so ambiguity creates duplicates.

### 8. ANTI-GOALS MUST BE DOMAIN-SPECIFIC
Anti-goals should identify real distractions specific to the idol's field:
- ❌ BAD: "Avoid procrastination" (generic, applies to everyone)
- ✅ GOOD: "Do NOT jump into advanced quantum mechanics before mastering classical mechanics and linear algebra — premature abstraction is the #1 killer of physics students"
- ✅ GOOD: "Do NOT chase hot stock tips or crypto — stick to understanding intrinsic value and reading annual reports"

### 9. USE THE GAP ANALYSIS
The gap analysis tells you exactly where the user is behind {idol_name}. Prioritize tasks that close the biggest gaps first. If the user is "beginner" in a category the idol excelled in, that category gets the most attention.

### 10. PROGRESSION STRUCTURE
The 12 weeks should follow a natural learning arc:
- **Weeks 1-3: Foundation** — Master prerequisites, build mental models, establish daily practice
- **Weeks 4-6: Core Skills** — Tackle the idol's primary domain skills, read foundational texts
- **Weeks 7-9: Applied Practice** — Build real things, solve real problems, create artifacts
- **Weeks 10-12: Integration** — Connect skills together, attempt idol-level challenges, reflect

Daily rhythm tasks should PROGRESS across weeks:
- Early weeks: simpler, shorter, foundational
- Middle weeks: more complex, longer, skill-building
- Late weeks: creative output, teaching others, synthesis

### 11. HYPER-PERSONALIZED
Use the User Context and Readiness data. Adapt difficulty to their level. If they're a beginner in the idol's domain, start with fundamentals — don't skip to advanced material.
